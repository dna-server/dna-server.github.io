Make a Free Book from Cardboard

1st, find a free book in .pdf format (or other) online or from a friend or at the library.


2nd, print the whole file as booklets, a number of pages that will be folded in half together. With this method, a 400 page book would require 100 sheets of printer paper (8.5x11 or whatever), and you would need to arrange the printed manuscript in twenty stacks of five pages that are folded into twenty booklets total.


3rd, the booklets are stapled or sewn on the center fold line. Try to put the pages against thick cardboard and staple them together vertically through the center crease, then pull the pages off of the cardboard and flatten the prongs onto the spine of the booklets with your nail or a flat butterknife etc.


4th, vise or clamp the pages together because the folded spine has given the pages too much bounce on that end. You want the whole thing here to be completely flat, so find a way to smash the bouncy spine together as you glue the stapled spines to a flat piece of cardboard like a cereal box that has been cut to match the dimensions of the spine.


5th, get a long rectangular piece of corrugated cardboard. Measure the size of the pages and cut out a wide and short piece that will serve as front cover, spine, and back cover when folded at the right places to form the spine. Then glue the flat cardboard spine of the pages to the inside of the folded corrugated cardboard spine. Glue the cover photo on or use a sharpie to write the title on the front and the outside of the spine.


Finally, make sure to either copy/print/paste this text inside the back cover and write the URL to this webpage (https://dna-server.github.io/cardboard-book.html) or your own version of it or both. As this book evolves, it would be good to have software that automatically adds just two or three pages which explain trash magic at the end of a file being printed. Or to simply print them separate and add them at step 4.



Free books can help to educate a lot of people quickly about obscure things.



WHAT IS TRASH MAGIC?

If we know how to make something useful and cool from free parts that are easy to get, we can make as many as we want, all for free.

If those items somehow hold their own blueprints or copying instructions, then the items become "self-replicating", like DNA.

They are built for free and given away for free. When a community of people publish all their free blueprints for building free stuff on a free website, then the entire library of blueprints is available anytime to anyone in that community or on that network.

Here's how to make your own free website:
- - - - -
Make a GitHub.com account (your username will be your website name), and upload your own blueprints to share with others. You can copy this website to use as a template by clicking the download buttons at the bottom.
- - - - -
Upload to a GitHub repository with the same name as your username. Your site will live at (YOUR_USERNAME).github.io and index.html will always be your homepage.
- - - - -
Show your friends so they can make their own. Add links to each others' websites.
