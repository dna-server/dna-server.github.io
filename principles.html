Statement of Principles

  • The purpose of technology is to create an environment in which all human needs are provided for for free to all humans.
  • A "technological complete set" is a collection of technologies which, together, provide this.
  • Any technology which is not part of a technological complete set is incomplete, and the highest priority for any technologist working on that technology is to complete it.
  • Technology should be built to minimize the number of humans and the amount of human labor required to complete a set, creating maximum personal freedom for each human to leave one set and join another.
  • A technological set cannot be considered to be complete unless it may be built from an existing stream of material, called the Trash Feed, which can be a combination of elements from the existing industrial system(waste) and from the natural world such as sunlight and rain.
  • The purpose of science is to create the strongest possible link between the physical world and the human mind.
  • Until such time as the technology a science is based on is complete, all science should be focused on completing the set, building the tools needed to finish that project prior to moving on to obscure studies.
  • In order for a science and technology set to be complete it must include the full process for teaching others, from scratch, the process of reconstructing that set on their own. A set which cannot reproduce itself using some form of education build into the set is not complete.
  • All professionalism is counter-productive to the goals of achieving a technological complete set. Creating and guarding professions such as "physicist", "artist", or "manager" is in direct opposition to making technology that can work for free. We must therefore oppose professionalism in all forms until such time as our technology forms a complete set, smashing down boundaries as needed to get things done. Artists must build circuits, and engineers must write poems.
  • We believe it is strictly necessary for all members of a society to be cared for in order for that society to claim that it is a good society. When basic necessities like food and shelter are withheld from humans for any reason, we see great suffering. We must ask ourselves how a society as "advanced" as ours still has this problem.
  • We glamorize creating technology that satisfies human needs and giving it away freely, with no bias in regards to the recipients' ideology, politics, behavior, religion, or any other specific characteristics. This is physical: it involves building actual things in the physical world and no amount of meetings, writings, elections or speeches can ever accomplish the needed tasks. This is a different type of thing than any of the other things in our society.
  • The ideological poison that has made our world so violent in an age of what should be infinite plenty is a worship of numbers. The belief that numbers can be used to quantify human value is based on the origins of our society in an extraction-based economy, and one based on human exploitation, and it involves a religious belief in the reality of numbers that is not upheld by obvious observations of Nature.
  • A path out of this trap is to exchange the hierarchies of linear counting with the practical, relational symmetries of geometry. This geometry must reject the mysticism of Plato and Euclid, and judge the quality of ideas on how well they help us build useful things and tell interesting stories.

WHAT IS TRASH MAGIC?

If we know how to make something useful and cool from free parts that are easy to get, we can make as many as we want, all for free.

If those items somehow hold their own blueprints or copying instructions, then the items become "self-replicating", like DNA.

They are built for free and given away for free. When a community of people publish all their free blueprints for building free stuff on a free website, then the entire library of blueprints is available anytime to anyone in that community or on that network.

Here's how to make your own free website:
- - - - -
Make a GitHub.com account (your username will be your website name), and upload your own blueprints to share with others. You can copy this website to use as a template by clicking the download buttons at the bottom.
- - - - -
Upload to a GitHub repository with the same name as your username. Your site will live at (YOUR_USERNAME).github.io and index.html will always be your homepage.
- - - - -
Show your friends so they can make their own. Add links to each others' websites.

Recycling and free-sharing have always been part of being human. But we are in a special period. For the first time in history, all the chemicals, technologies and materials that we need in order to live well (as well as the tools needed in order to work with them) are spread all over the globe. Almost anyone can get almost anything almost anywhere.

The biggest change of all, however, is digital networks. We now have a great density of intercommunication and material transport (plus overflowing waste streams and pollution as a result). But being online doesn't have to cost money. Three people with any sort of wifi circuit or LoRa chip can make their own network for free. So can three hundred, three thousand, or three million.

Our new internet ought to be a digital archive of all the things that matter to us IN THE REAL WORLD. The new internet becomes like an augmented-reality-game-map, showing users all the places around town they can meet cool people or get free stuff. The free stuff has a label that links to its website on the library-map showing how it replicates itself for free. The new internet should simply be a digital library/map of the free community, having dedicated pages for all important physical things like rivers, dumpsters, and farms. The purpose of the library/map is to help people live good lives WITHOUT SPENDING MONEY.

Trading USB drives with each other is a kind of internet. Share memes. Design social circuits. Create physical social media. Instead of a post online, trying sharing a cool or useful thing with a real person irl. Even better if that thing contains its own instructions for making more of itself for free!

All of this can be done while still living with capitalism and obeying the law, making it a good option for everyone to participate in. Additionally, every time anyone adds a single blueprint to their personal library, it makes it easier for everyone else in the network to live for free. All of the items and places point back to the online pages where their blueprints are shared. This is the same network on which all the rest of our blueprints are shared, just like DNA in any cell contains the blueprints to recreate all the rest of your body.

If they can repeat this process consistently, they have designed a social circuit. It is the social circuits which connect us and make up our new free world, not just the online networks. We want everything to be free for everyone, everywhere, immediately. The free world will recycle the capitalist world the way that fungus recycles a log.

Don't assume it would be impossible to build things just because there's no money circulating. If the people who provided the materials and the people who built it catch the vision of Trash Magic, then it can be built for free consistently. Everyone in the Trash Magic network is incentivized to do and give what they can for free because they themselves are benefitting so much from others' free gifts to them.
